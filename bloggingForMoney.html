4. Choosing your Theme

Past the content, the layout of feel of your website will have the biggest impact on your success and visitor experience. I am sure you have been to a blog or Wordpress site that felt cheaply made and uninviting. It may have had confusing menus or bad links. Most likely, it looked plain and nothing stood out. Luckily, today there are thousands of free, responsive Wordpress themes available for everyone to use. 5. Building the Basics

A good foundation will allow you spread your wings. There are four pages that any successful Wordpress blog includes to ensure that Google likes them in addition to their readers. These pages lay the groundwork for a XML Sitemap, which is a must in getting indexed through search engines. In addition to being needed for the techie stuff, they give your reader a chance to learn a bit about whats behind the domain name. Real people like real people... The "Must Have" pages for any website are:
About Me 
This is where you put a face to the website you have created. This is not an about the website page. You are the focus of this content. So talk about yourself, your qualifications, how you got started in this niche, what your hobbies and goals are. What are your dreams? All of these topics go into creating the personality that people will think of when reading your content. The less faceless you seem the less cold your visitors will be. People like understanding who is giving them advice. I am sure you are much more likely to listen to the advice of a friend or colleague, over that of a random passerby on the street. The goal is between 500-1000 words on who you are and how you got to where you are today doing this website. For ideas check out my About Me. I also recommend looking at other websites in your niche, to get a feel for how they write their About Me pages.
Overview or Mission
This in its most simple for is nothing more than a mission statement or end goal for why this website is up. It can be seen as an about me for your business. This is the page that people who come to your website go to see if they can gain value from being there. What things will you be covering in your blog?  What problem are you fixing by creating this website? When all is said and done, what will your readers learn and come away with? This webpage will be very specific to your chosen niche. Given that circumstance, it is not good advice to say write this or write that. What I will say is if you answer the above questions, you will have a well thought out and organized Overview page. For an example to draw formatting ideas from here is my What We Do page.  
Niche specific page to organize all categories and posts
This is the top level page for your niche posts. This can be an introduction page for a specific part of your niche, or you may have a few as I do to cover the broader categories within your niche. However you decide to organize it, these pages should be a good starting point for your readers and should be linked to from the Overview page. Having these internal links creates a structure to the site and allows an easier time for the visitor to navigate quickly between pages. In this page I would focus on going more into detail about the specifics within your niche that you are planning to cover with posts in the future. This gives the reader a reason to come back in the future. When you properly break down your niche for the reader, you are providing groundwork for the growth and development of your site down the road. Having a good foundation is key, and a recurring theme when beginning blogging with the intent to make money. For examples of how these are written check out Music Production Gear and Fund Your Passions.

10. A Plan Moving Forward

It is important with any endeavor, to have a solid plan of attack. When committing to blogging and making money with it you must set up for long term success. This is a trickle, stream, river type of industry. In this case it means setting goals, and target achievements. This last step requires some participation. In order to make your goals real and make it harder for you back out on them, write your goals down. In the comments below write out your goals for:
  • 30 days out
  • 90 days out
  • 6 months out
  • 1 year out
It is important to phrase your goals as if you have completed them already. This is a neat little psychology trick I learned from mentors over the years. This is so that when you read them each day you are literally thinking of them as a reality. As you will learn about down the road, your thoughts control your reality. So for example DON'T = I will have a website with ten posts by six months out DO = After six months of website developement, I have posted ten articles. The difference is subtle but present none the less. There is a separate article covering goals setting and the S.M.A.R.T. technique in the works. Stay tuned for growth and updates.
