Albert Einstein Success Quote Of The Day March 10, 2017

"Intellectual growth should commence at birth and cease only at death." - Albert Einstein

This man needs no introduction. Albert Einstein is a mind beyond its time. His accomplishments have propelled human technology into a new era. Albeit, primarily warfare. His ability to envision the abstract and unknown has allowed civilization to progress consistently since his time. His growth never slowed and never ceded. He was driven to continually develop and sharpen his mind throughout his entire life. He attributes that to a vast majority of his success. If he had not been able to learn and absorb from birth to death, the world would not be nearly the same. Overcoming learning disabilities as a child, Albert Einstein has lived the words he spoke. Through and through, he is a man of character and success like few who have ever lived. His accomplishments will be remembered and revered for centuries if not millennia to come.

So what does this mean?

It means never stop learning. Engage at all levels in life. Be present and soak up the moments. Experience breeds knowledge and applying that knowledge is power. Success breaths it's life into those who constantly grow and develop. Back when I was in the Marines, we had a saying about staying alive. In a less lethal sense it still applies here.

"Complacency kills." - Every drill instructor ever

It is true in combat, but this principle holds true in life as well. Think about it. If Steve Jobs had dropped out of college and that was it. He never learned more about his passions. Never met up with Steve Wozniak. No iPod. No Macbook. And there is a good chance that many other competitors to Apple would not have advanced so much due to a stark lack of competition.

Albert Einstein is relating the importance of continual growth the entirety of your life. From the moment you take your first breath, you are soaking in the world. It isn't until most people are adults, that they think they know what they need to and become stubborn and complacent. These are dangerous waters to tempt.
